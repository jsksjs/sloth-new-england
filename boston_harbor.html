Boston Harbor

You may be asking: Why should I go to Boston Harbor, instead of any other harbor out there? What makes Boston Harbor so special?

Well, the answers are, you shouldn't, and, nothing. The most-often cited reason why Boston Harbor is better than the thousands of other harbors out there is its history. Like that one time 243 years ago, when a bunch of Englishmen dressed as Native Americans threw tea into the harbor, or that time in 1919 when there was a literal flood of molasses.

But that all happened so long ago. Boston Harbor does NOT taste like tea, and it absolutely does NOT taste like molasses. It tastes like GARBAGE. Don't drink the water.

So really, all the cool things that used to make Boston harbor interesting are long-gone. I don't know what the biological half-life of tea or molasses is, but today, you'll see neither racist cosplayers nor this cool scene depicted by the Boston Globe of the time:

"Molasses, waist deep, covered the street and swirled and bubbled about the wreckage ... Here and there struggled a form‍—‌whether it was animal or human being was impossible to tell. Only an upheaval, a thrashing about in the sticky mass, showed where any life was ... Horses died like so many flies on sticky fly-paper. The more they struggled, the deeper in the mess they were ensnared. Human beings‍—‌men and women‍—‌suffered likewise."

With that gone, there is no reason to visit Boston for its port. Go there for any number of other legitimate attractions that aren't ports.
